ABOUT

Fallback was established in 2005 based out of Fargo, North Dakota. Fallback consisted of 5 members with their own unique influences which cover every inch of the spectrum to create music that appeals on every level. Fallback's sound is emotionally direct, raw, and captivating.

Lead singer Jarrod Simek has a vocal range that can go hauntingly deep and melodic to infuriated growls in a matter of seconds. The guitarist duo of Cody Marshall and Tyler Strand do an amazing job of switching between rhythm and lead throughout the band, effectively feeding off each other's beefy power chord-driven rhythm riffs with intricate lead harmonies.

Dustin Huber filled in the shoes of original bassist Addison Peck, bringing new insight and forging an influential bass style that came into play with the band's newest material. Tessa Breitbach replaced original drummer Mike Lund — a "no mercy" drummer who'd been playing for 11 years, holding nothing back, establishing a new respect for the band and female drummers.

Fallback released their self-titled album debut in May 2008, which spawned great singles including "Driven" (which hit the Top Ten Loud City Charts) and fan favorite "Visegrip" which became a staple of their live show. Their biggest stage moment came opening for Five Finger Death Punch in front of 2,500 people. In 2009, Fallback won Q98's Battle of the Bands and was selected as one of 15 bands nationally for the Rockstar Mayhem Festival Pick The Band contest. Their follow-up EP "Vexing the Dull" was released independently in 2010 to strong critical reception, earning airplay on New Rock 105.1's Iowa Unsigned and interest from Lazer 103.3 in Des Moines.

PRESS & FEATURES

"Driven" — Top Ten Loud City Charts

The lead single from the self-titled album broke into the Top Ten on the Loud City Charts.

The Muse's Muse — "Vexing the Dull" Review

10/10 Technical, 10/10 Overall Talent, 10/10 Performance. Reviewer Cyrus Rhodes: "an impressive musical production from start to finish." (Nov 2010)

Q98 Battle of the Bands — 2009 Champions

Fallback won Q98's Battle of the Bands Finals on April 7, 2009 at the Old Broadway, competing against Lucyd, Go Steve Jones, and A Dying Tide.

Rockstar Mayhem Festival — Pick The Band Contest

Selected as one of 15 bands nationally for the Pick The Band - Rockstar Mayhem Festival contest (June 2009). Performance date was July 31st in Cleveland, OH.

New Rock 105.1 — Iowa Unsigned

Featured on Benny Black's Iowa Unsigned show on New Rock 105.1 in Des Moines. All five Vexing the Dull tracks were submitted and praised by the host. (Sept 2010)

Lazer 103.3 — Local Licks

Suzi Lazer at Des Moines Radio Group's Lazer 103.3 requested Fallback's press release and demo for the Local Licks program. (Sept 2010)

Streetcult Sampler Vol. 9 — Eclipse Records

"Driven" was featured as Track #1 on the Streetcult Street Teams Loud Music Sampler CD Volume 9, released by Eclipse Records / Streetcult Marketing (2008).

ESP Guitars — Endorsement Submission

Submitted a demo and press kit to ESP Guitars for possible endorsement consideration (Aug 2008).

TAXI A&R — Song Critique

Through Disc Makers, received a professional song critique from TAXI's A&R staff, the world's leading independent A&R company.

Northern Edge Music — Interview

Interview published on northernedgemusic.info (Oct 2008). Northern Edge Music was a key local music publication covering the Fargo-Moorhead scene.

Lex and Terry Morning Show

Featured on the nationally syndicated Lex and Terry Morning Show for unsigned talent. Aired August 25, 2008 — confirmed by fan comments on MySpace that morning.

Q98 Radio Promotion

Worked with Kelly Ring at Go Radio Broadcasting (Q98) on radio ads promoting the Five Finger Death Punch pre-show at Rascals (May 2008). Novack from Q98 facilitated the connection.

525.com & BreakthruRadio.com

Fallback actively promoted listener requests on 525.com and BreakthruRadio.com.

CD Baby & iTunes Distribution

Self-titled album distributed digitally through CD Baby and iTunes, generating ongoing sales and direct ACH payments to the band.

Hard Rock Society — Fargo/Moorhead

Regularly featured in Scot's Hard Rock Society newsletter, one of the key promoters of the Fargo-Moorhead metal scene. Fallback was a mainstay of the local circuit at Rick's Bar, The Nestor Tavern, and The Aquarium.

True Talent Management

Connected with True Talent Management through local promoter "Hat" for professional music business guidance and industry contacts (Nov 2007).

Sonicbids EPK
